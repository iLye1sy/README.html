Ronnie Coleman

Ronnie Coleman is a retired American professional bodybuilder who is widely considered one of the greatest bodybuilders of all time.

He was born on May 13, 1964, in Monroe, Louisiana, and began weightlifting at the age of 13. He attended Grambling State University on a football scholarship and graduated with a degree in accounting.

Coleman won the Mr. Olympia title eight years in a row, from 1998 to 2005, tying the record set by Lee Haney. He also won numerous other bodybuilding competitions throughout his career.

After retiring from bodybuilding, Coleman has undergone multiple surgeries and procedures to address injuries sustained during his career. He has also become a motivational speaker and has released a line of nutritional supplements.

Despite his retirement, Ronnie Coleman remains an icon in the bodybuilding world and his dedication to the sport has inspired countless athletes.
